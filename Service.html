Digital Printing

Paper Stock:

  • Coated Paper
     Glossy, matte, or silk finishes for sharp images.

  • Regular paper
     More natural, absorbent, and ideal for writing.

  • Cardstock
     Thicker, used for business cards and invitations.

Ink types:

  • Toner-based ink
     Powdered ink fused onto paper using heat.

  • Inkjet ink
     Liquid ink sprayed onto paper.

  • UV-curable ink
     Cured with UV light for fast drying and durability.

Book Binding

Read More

  • Perfect Binding
     A glued spine method for softcover books and magazines, offering a clean and professional look.

  • Saddle Stitching
     Uses staples along the fold to bind booklets and catalogs, making it a cost-effective and durable option.

  • Spiral & Wire Binding
     Pages are held together with a coil or wire loops, allowing easy flipping and a flat-lay design, perfect for notebooks, reports, and manuals.

Laminating

Read More

  • Glossy & Matte Lamination
     A durable coating that enhances appearance—glossy for a vibrant shine, matte for a smooth, non-reflective finish. Ideal for posters, menus, and certificates.

  • Encapsulation
     A thicker lamination that completely seals the document between two plastic layers, offering waterproof and tear-resistant protection, perfect for IDs and important documents.
